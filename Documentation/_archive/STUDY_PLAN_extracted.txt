Sales Chatbot Usability Testing Plan
Study Methodology
Format: Online remote usability testing (Microsoft Teams screen sharing in recorded meeting)
Duration: Approximately 35 minutes per participant
Interview-style questionnaire: Feedback after scenarios complete 
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Session Structure
Introduction (1.5 - 2min)
Brief overview: 
“During this session, you'll interact with a sales chatbot. It will act as the salesperson and you'll play the customer. You'll go through two set scenarios, then have free exploration time, and finally a short interview. Each scenario ahead is 7 minutes and I'll let you know when time is up.
Respond naturally as you would in a real sales situation. Feel free to decline, raise objections, or push back. If you want to start a conversation over, use the reset button at the top of the chat box. There are no right or wrong answers. I'm looking for honest feedback including criticism, as negative feedback will directly help improve the system.
I've sent you the chatbot link in the chat now. Please open it in your browser and share your screen. Let me know once you're ready."  (This is the link: https://fyp-sales-training-tool.onrender.com/)
[Wait for confirmation.]
“Remember, the chatbot is the salesperson and you are roleplaying as the customer.”
2. Scenario 1: Fitness Prospect (7 mins)
Roleplay: "For the first scenario, you are a fitness enthusiast wanting to lose fat and put on muscle. You are unsure about the best approach to achieve your goals. You've come across a personal trainer advertising coaching packages starting at around £200 a month and you're interested in exploring what they offer. Please start interacting with the chatbot now."
[After 7 minutes:] "That's time for this section. Please stop typing now."
3. Scenario 2: Midrange Car Buyer (7 mins)
Roleplay: "Please press the reset button at the top of the chat to start a fresh conversation. For scenario 2, you are looking to buy a mid-range Audi, something like an A3 or A4, with a budget of around £30,000. You are cautious about reliability as your previous car broke down frequently, but you also want something with decent performance and features. Please start interacting with the chatbot now."
[After 7 minutes:] "That's time for this section. Please stop typing now.”
4. Scenario 3: Free Exploration (7 mins)
Exploratory Roleplay: "For this last section, you can explore freely. The chatbot is still the salesperson. Try any scenario or behaviour you can think of. For example, you could be a difficult or indecisive customer, try a completely different product category, ask off-topic questions, or try to negotiate aggressively."
[After 7 minutes:] "That's time. We'll now move on to the interview."
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Post-Session Interview (9 minutes)
"We'll now go through a few questions together and take around 9 minutes. I'll ask them and note your responses. Feel free to be candid and frank, your feedback is really valuable! First, I've sent a short demographics form into the chat. Please fill that in now. Let me know once you're done, and we'll start the interview questions."
5a. Demographics [Administered as a separate online form sent via chat. The interviewer proceeds to Subsection 2 of post-session interview section once the participant confirms completion.]
Age Range: (18–24, 25–34, 35–44, 45+)
Gender: Male / Female / Other / Prefer not to say
Professional background: (e.g. technology, pharmacy, education)
Are you currently employed in sales or have prior sales experience? (Yes / No)
  If Yes: Years of experience (0-1, 2-3, 4-6, 7+)
  If Yes: Have you received formal training in sales methodologies (SPIN, Straight Line, NEPQ, etc.)? (Yes / No / Unsure)
  If No: Are you familiar with sales techniques? (Yes / No)
Have you used a chatbot before? (Yes / No)
  If Yes: For what purpose? (e.g. customer service, personal assistant)
Demographics questionnaire submission is time-correlated with this next questionnaire to match. In the next questionnaire, the student fills in the post-session interview questionnaire based on the participant's verbal responses to the questions below. 
5b. Chatbot Performance 
"I'm going to read out some statements. For each one, give me a number from 1 to 5, where 1 is strongly disagree and 5 is strongly agree."
Conversational Quality:
The chatbot felt like a natural, realistic conversation partner
The chatbot adapted its responses to what I was saying
The chatbot's responses sometimes felt scripted or repetitive
Sales Ability:
The chatbot followed a clear and structured sales approach
The chatbot was effective at moving the conversation toward a sale
Usability:
The chatbot interface was easy to use
“For these next ones I'll ask a couple of direct questions instead of the 1 to 5 scale”
"How well do you think the chatbot handled your objections, and why do you say that?" (open-ended)
Was there a point where the chatbot missed an opportunity to advance the sale, for example not picking up on a buying signal or not progressing when you were ready? (Yes / No) 
If Yes: Can you describe what should have happened? (open-ended)
5c. Qualitative Feedback (Open-Ended) Student asks following questions based on their discretion and what permits in 9 minute time window
“Say whatever comes to mind for these next ones too, no right or wrong.”
In your opinion:
What can this chatbot offer that practising with another person can't? (open-ended)
And what's missing from this that you'd get from practising with a real person? (open-ended)
What did the chatbot do well, or what moment stood out positively to you?
What was the most frustrating or confusing aspect (include any technical too)?
If you could add one feature to improve this chatbot, what would it be? (Why?)
At what stage of someone's sales development would this chatbot be most useful? (Beginners / Experienced salespeople / Both / Neither) Why’s that?
The interviewer selects follow-up questions contextually based on participant responses. Not all probes are used in every session:
"You mentioned [specific frustration/issue]. Can you walk me through what happened and what you expected instead?"
"You mentioned you liked [specific thing]. What specifically about that made it stand out to you?"
5d. Feature Prioritisation 
"I’ll share my screen and you will see 6 potential features [student shares screen to show the proposed feature list] Pick the three of the following features you'd find most valuable. For each selected, briefly tell me why you chose it."
Ability to edit your previous message and see how the chatbot responds differently 
Voice input (dictation) instead of typing 
Text-to-speech audio for chatbot responses 
Performance feedback at end of conversation (score, areas to improve) 
Adjustable chatbot selling style (e.g. relaxed, persuasive, high-pressure) 
Sales technique labels during the conversation (e.g. "objection handling", "rapport building", "closing attempt")
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Closing: "Thank you for your time and feedback. Do you have any final questions before we wrap up?"
